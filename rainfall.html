Rainfall Prediction in NZ Using ML

Dataset Preprocessing part |

Training part

This study focuses on the development and evaluation of machine learning (ML) models for daily rainfall prediction in the regions of Aotearoa New Zealand. Rainfall prediction plays a critical role in areas such as agriculture, water management and natural disaster prevention. The main objective of the study is to develop and test ML models for accurate rainfall forecasting, evaluate their performance and select the most accurate model.

The study uses the Design Science Research methodology, which involves the iterative creation and evaluation of artifacts - in this case, ML models. The work involved extracting meteorological data from the Cliflo database and pre-processing it, including recovery of missing values and creation of new features. The developed models, including Random Forest, Gradient-Boosted, XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost and LSTM, underwent three stages of training where the best time lags and hyperparameters were selected. The results show that the models are able to accurately predict precipitation. The LSTM model with a time lag of 30 days showed the best results in terms of RMSE, MAE and R² metrics, outperforming the other models. Limitations of the study include limits on the amount of data and available resources. Prospects for further research include extending forecast time horizons and integrating data from other sources to improve forecast accuracy.
